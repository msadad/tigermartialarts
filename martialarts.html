Martial Arts

Our Martial Arts Programs

Explore the unique benefits of each program we offer.

Karate

Overview: Karate is a striking martial art that emphasizes powerful punches, kicks, and blocks combined with kata (prearranged forms) and sparring. Training builds mental discipline, focus, and physical fitness.

History: Originating from Okinawa, Japan, Karate has a rich tradition and philosophy emphasizing self-control and respect.

Benefits: Develops strength, agility, coordination, and self-confidence while improving mental focus and discipline.

Class Structure: Classes typically start with warm-up exercises, followed by technique drills, kata practice, and controlled sparring sessions.

Suitable For: All ages and fitness levels, especially those interested in traditional striking arts and self-discipline.

Join us and unlock your inner strength and precision through Karate!

Judo

Overview: Judo focuses on throws, grappling, joint locks, and pins. It teaches how to use leverage and balance to control opponents.

History: Founded in Japan by Jigoro Kano in 1882, Judo is now an Olympic sport and a global discipline.

Benefits: Enhances balance, coordination, cardiovascular fitness, and mental resilience.

Class Structure: Warm-up, technique demonstrations, drilling throws and pins, followed by randori (free practice).

Suitable For: Those who enjoy close-contact combat, grappling, and strategic fighting methods.

Discover the power of balance and leverage with Judo.

Jiu-Jitsu

Overview: Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a ground-based martial art emphasizing submissions such as chokes and joint locks.

History: Developed in Brazil from traditional Japanese Jujutsu and Judo, it focuses on technique over brute strength.

Benefits: Improves flexibility, strategic thinking, endurance, and self-defence skills.

Class Structure: Warm-up, technique instruction, drilling, positional sparring, and live rolling (sparring).

Suitable For: Anyone interested in effective ground combat and submission techniques, regardless of size.

Master the art of control and technique with Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu.

Muay Thai

Overview: Known as the “Art of Eight Limbs,” Muay Thai uses punches, elbows, knees, and shin strikes.

History: Thailand’s national sport and martial art with roots going back hundreds of years.

Benefits: Builds cardiovascular endurance, strength, power, and practical self-defence skills.

Class Structure: Intense warm-up, pad work, technique drills, clinching practice, and conditioning exercises.

Suitable For: Those seeking a high-intensity striking martial art with real-world applications.

Build power and resilience with authentic Muay Thai training.

Self Defence

Overview: Practical, real-world techniques designed to protect you in everyday situations.

Focus: Situational awareness, de-escalation tactics, quick responses, and effective escape techniques.

Benefits: Increased personal safety, confidence, and ability to react calmly under pressure.

Class Structure: Scenario-based drills, technique practice, awareness training, and legal considerations.

Suitable For: Everyone — men, women, and youth — who want to feel safer and more prepared.

Empower yourself with skills that could save your life.

Kids Classes

Overview: Structured martial arts sessions for young learners to build physical and mental skills.

Benefits: Develops confidence, respect, focus, coordination, and social skills in a fun environment.

Class Structure: Warm-ups, games, basic technique drills, and age-appropriate sparring or partner work.

Suitable For: Children aged 4-12 of all fitness levels and backgrounds.

Help your child grow stronger and more confident through martial arts.

Open Mat

Overview: Unstructured time for students to practice techniques, spar, or roll freely.

Benefits: Reinforces learning, builds confidence, and fosters community among practitioners.

Structure: Participants bring their own goals — drill techniques, roll with partners, or get feedback from instructors.

Suitable For: All students who want extra practice and peer learning.

Use Open Mat time to refine your skills at your own pace.
